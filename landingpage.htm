Objective

The core of this service builds on the European Climate Assessment & Dataset (ECA&D) and E-OBS datasets for Europe. ECA&D and E-OBS will be transformed into an operational system, delivering regularly updated gridded products based on European in-situ data for many Essential Climate Variables (ECVs). The underlying station data that include surface air temperature, precipitation, humidity, wind speed and direction, will be made available as well, pending permission by the owners of these data. To serve climate change monitoring and climate impact assessments, a large number of user-oriended climate indices will be provided, both as time series at station sites and as gridded products. Monthly and annual state-of-the-European-climate reports will be produces from the pan-European and regional datasets, including the climate indices and uncertainty information.

The following subjects will be improved through this service:
  • E-OBS grid spacing (0.1° by 0.1° versus 0.25° by 0.25°)
  • Quality control procedures
  • Homogenization of time series
  • Gridding methods
  • New (gridded) indices will be provided

Datasets

This service will provide and/or make use of the following pan-European and regional datasets:
  • E-OBS dataset (1950-2017)
    • Daily minimum, mean, maximum temperature, precipitation, sea level pressure over Europe (existing, will be updated)
    • Humidity/dew point temperature, radiation sums, daily averaged wind speed over Europe (to be produced)
  • Nordic Gridded Climate Dataset (NGCD, 1981-2017)
    • Daily mean temperature and precipitation over Fennoscandia (existing, will be updated)
  • Long term Nordic Gridded Climate Dataset (NGCD_Rec, ~1950-2017)
    • Monthly precipitation over Fennoscandia (to be produced)
  • HISTALP_Rec (~1900-2017)
    • Monthly precipitation over Alpine region (existing, will be updated)
  • Alpine precipitation grid dataset (APGD, 1971-2008)
    • Daily precipitation over Alpine region (static)
  • CarpatClim (1961-2010)
    • Daily dataset for, among others, minimum, mean and maximum temperature, precipitation, wind speed, relative humidity and global radiation (static)
The lead contractor is the Royal Netherlands Meteorological Institute (KNMI). KNMI is the Dutch national meteorological agency. It has a long record in analysing and processing of in-situ station observations, mainly through involvement in many EU-funded research projects focussing on ECA&D and E-OBS. These data are also used as input in projects focusing on climate services.
